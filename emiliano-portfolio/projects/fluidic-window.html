Smart Materials · Harvard SEAS

Smart fluidic window energy regulation

Liquid-flow modules for orthogonal daylight and thermal conductance tuning in building envelopes.

Overview

At Harvard SEAS, this research focused on smart fluidic structures that can regulate optical and thermal behavior using liquid flow cells.

Design approach

The work included characterizing liquids, building thermal/optical testing setups, and evaluating modular device concepts for energy-efficient building systems.
